Personal Finance — beginner notes

Budgeting
- A budget is a plan for your money: income minus expenses.
- Income is money you earn from a job. Take-home pay is income after taxes.
- Expenses are money you spend, split into needs (rent, food, bills) and wants (eating out, subscriptions).
- The 50/30/20 rule splits money into 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings.
- If expenses are less than income, the leftover becomes savings.

Saving
- Savings is money you set aside instead of spending.
- An emergency fund is savings that covers 3 to 6 months of expenses, for surprises like losing a job.
- Build an emergency fund before you start investing.

Interest
- Interest is the cost of borrowing money, or the reward for saving it.
- Principal is the original amount of money you save or borrow.
- The interest rate is the percentage charged or earned on the principal.
- Compound interest means earning interest on your interest, not just on the principal. It depends on the principal, the interest rate, and time. The longer the money sits, the faster it grows, so small amounts snowball over many years.

Debt and credit
- Debt is money you owe, paid back with interest.
- A credit card lets you borrow short-term, and the APR is the yearly interest rate on what you owe.
- Good debt like a mortgage can build value, while bad debt like a high-interest credit card balance drains you.
- A credit score is a number showing how reliably you repay debt, and paying on time raises it.

Investing
- Investing means putting money into things that can grow, like stocks.
- Index funds spread money across many companies, which is called diversification and lowers risk.
- Risk versus return means higher potential returns usually come with higher risk.
- Compound interest is why investing early matters, because time does most of the work.
- Retirement accounts like a 401(k) or IRA let investments grow over the long term.

Inflation and net worth
- Inflation is prices rising over time, so money buys less later.
- Savings need to outpace inflation, which is one reason people invest.
- Net worth is what you own (assets) minus what you owe (liabilities).Personal Finance — beginner notes
